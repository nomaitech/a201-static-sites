II — The Strokes

How we paint
has changed.
The art hasn't.

Every painting, every codebase, follows the same ancient rhythm. The tools evolve. The gesture endures.

I
The Sketch / First Commit

The rough idea, unafraid

No painter starts with perfection. The first marks are bold, clumsy, alive. Your first commit should be too — an honest record of the idea, not an attempt to impress.

II
Broad Strokes / Fast Builds

Paint the whole before the part

Lay in the big shapes first. With AI as your brush-arm, you can cover enormous canvas in an afternoon. The composition must sing before details are earned.

III
Step Back / Code Review

Distance reveals what proximity hides

Walk away from the easel. Read your diff as a stranger would. The problem is never in the line you're staring at — it's in the relationship between lines.

IV
Detail Work / Refinement

Polish only what the eye will find

Detail work comes last, and only where it matters. The corner no viewer will ever study doesn't need glazing. Ship the canvas with the details that earn attention.

III — The Canvas
Before AI, you needed a week to paint a portrait.
Now you can paint a gallery in an afternoon.

The canvas was always infinite. The limitation was the painter's arm — how fast it could move, how long before fatigue. Modern tools have extended that arm. Not replaced the painter. Extended it.

The blank screen still demands the same courage it always has. What changes is how quickly your vision can escape your mind and exist in the world. That speed is not a shortcut. It is a gift. Use it to iterate more, not think less.

IV — The Palette

Every colour
is a choice.

The painter reaches for the right pigment. Every tool, every language, every framework is a colour. Know what each one does. Choose deliberately.

The Fine Brush

TypeScript — Prussian Blue

Precise, controlled, unforgiving of careless marks. For when the composition demands exact lines. It slows you down just enough to make you think before each stroke.

The Broad Stroke

Python — Raw Ochre

Expressive, immediate, warm. Great for laying in the underpainting — the logic skeleton before the polish. Fast strokes that reveal the structure.

The Ground Layer

SQL — Burnt Umber

Invisible when done right. The primer that holds everything else. Nothing good is built without a well-prepared ground. You'll regret skipping this layer.

The Light Source

AI Tools — Titanium White

Light illuminates everything, but overuse flattens the painting. AI is the brightest pigment in the modern palette. A little lifts the whole work. Too much and you lose the depth.

V — The Manifesto
  • Ship like you paint.
  • Boldly. Iteratively. Without apology.
  • Every commit is a brushstroke.
  • Every deploy, a canvas revealed.
  • The masterpiece is in the making.
