Artists

Visual makers in
the collective.

Generative artists, illustrators, photographers, designers and image-makers who use AI to extend what they can see and make.

"The AI doesn't have a vision. It has patterns. My job is to find the thing that shouldn't be there — the result that surprises even the model — and follow it."
Maya Chen · Generative Artist

Practice areas

Generative Art
Process-based image-making

Using code, prompts, and generative systems to make images that emerge from rules rather than direct intention. The surprise is the point.

Visual Design
Identity & systems design

Using AI to explore vast spaces of visual possibility for logos, brands and design systems — then making the choices that matter by hand.

Photography
AI-augmented photography

From AI-enhanced darkroom techniques to prompt-guided compositions to training models on personal archives. Photography extended, not replaced.

Illustration
Digital & hybrid illustration

Illustrators who use AI to generate rough ideas, textures or colour studies — then bring their own hand and eye to finish the work.

Motion
Generative motion design

Using AI for character animation, video synthesis and real-time visual systems. The frame is still authored. The motion is a collaboration.

Spatial
Installation & spatial work

Physical/digital work where AI responds to space, movement or presence. The room itself becomes a collaborator.

M
Maya Chen
Generative Art · Sound

Maya works at the intersection of sound and visual space. Her practice centres on what cities sound like — not the sounds you notice but the ones underneath. Every City Has a Frequency is her most ambitious piece to date: a generative soundscape for every London borough, trained on three years of her own field recordings.

How she uses AI

"I use AI to find patterns in the recordings I've made — not to generate the sound, but to find structure in what I've already captured. The model shows me things I wasn't listening for."

S
Sam Okafor
Identity Design · Systems

Sam makes visual systems that evolve. His Theseus project is a generative identity that rebuilds itself daily, trained on protest typography from five decades of London political movements.

How he uses AI

"AI gives me a thousand versions in the time it used to take me to make one. My job is to know which one to keep."

M
Marcus Bell
Typography · Digital Art

Marcus has strong opinions about fonts and even stronger opinions about what happens when we stop using them. Font Grief — his AI-generated obituaries for discontinued typefaces — is his most personal project.

How he uses AI

"I use language models to write the grief. The typography is always mine."

A
Aisha Osei
Textile Art · Generative Pattern

Aisha generates weaving patterns from satellite imagery of West African markets, then produces them on a Jacquard loom. The AI reads landscape as structure. She translates it into cloth.

How she uses AI

"It can see the geometry in an aerial photograph that I can't. I can feel what the fabric should be. Together we get somewhere neither of us could reach."

J
Jess Wu
Colour Systems · Creative Code

Jess is obsessed with colour — specifically with what happens when you train a model on centuries of painted colour and ask it to invent new palettes. Daily Colour Theory is her third generative colour project and the first one she's happy with.

How she uses AI

"The palette is a starting point. I still have to decide which colours mean something and which are just noise."

O
Oliver Crane
Photography · Archive

Oliver has been photographing London pubs since 2018 — and training a model on all of them since 2023. He uses it to generate the pub that used to exist on a corner before it became a Pret. It's about memory as much as architecture.

How he uses AI

"I'm trying to photograph places that are already gone. The AI helps me lie in a way that tells the truth."
