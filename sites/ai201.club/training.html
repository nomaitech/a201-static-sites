Workshops

Learn alongside fellow makers.

Practical sessions where members teach members. Not tutorials on prompting — workshops on using AI to go further with the work you already do.

We don't teach AI.
We use it together.

Every workshop at AI201 is led by a practitioner who has actually used a specific AI technique in their own work. Not someone who read about it — someone who built something with it and hit the interesting problems.

We keep sessions small (max 12 people), hands-on, and focused on a specific outcome: leaving with something you made, not a list of things you learned about.

What we run

01
Generative Art

Hands-On Generative Art

From p5.js to Stable Diffusion to ControlNet — practical sessions where you make images, animations and interactive pieces using AI as a generative collaborator. No prior coding experience needed.

02
Music Production

AI-Assisted Music Production

How working musicians and producers actually use AI tools in their workflow. Covers generative composition, stem separation, audio-to-MIDI, and how to use AI to break through creative blocks without handing over creative control.

03
Writing

Writing With (Not For) AI

For novelists, poets, screenwriters and anyone who works with language. How to use language models as a collaborator that generates material you react to — not a ghostwriter that removes you from the work.

04
Creative Coding

Build Something in a Day

A full-day session where you design, build and ship a creative tool or interactive piece with AI assistance. You arrive with an idea, you leave with a URL. Hosted quarterly.

05
All disciplines

Open Practice Session

No agenda. Bring your current project, your current problem, or your current confusion. Facilitated but loose — a space to work alongside other people using AI in their practice.

How our workshops work

Small groups

Maximum 12 people per session. Enough for a conversation, small enough for everyone to get help when they need it.

Practitioner-led

Every facilitator is a working artist, musician or maker who uses AI in their actual practice — not an instructor with a slide deck.

Free or pay-what-you-can

Most sessions are free. Occasional specialist sessions have a suggested contribution. Nobody is turned away for cost.
