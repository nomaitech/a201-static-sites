// The vibecoding way

Code with
pure vibes.

No specs. No sprints. No Jira tickets. Just you, an AI, and the overwhelming urge to ship something beautiful (or beautifully cursed) into the world.

noun / verb / lifestyle
vibecoding
/vaɪb·koʊ.dɪŋ/
The practice of building software by describing what you want to an AI and letting it generate the code, while you guide the direction based on intuition, aesthetics, and pure creative energy. Coined by Andrej Karpathy in 2025. Popularised by weirdos who realised you don't need to know what a for-loop is to build something incredible.

"I didn't write a single line of code. I just described the vibe and it appeared."
// The pillars

What makes a vibecoder

It's not about the code. It's about the energy.

🧠

Vibe over syntax

You don't memorise APIs. You describe what you want and the AI figures out the implementation. Your job is the creative vision, not the semicolons.

🚀

Ship over perfect

A deployed mess beats an unshipped masterpiece. Vibecoding is about momentum — get it out there, see what happens, iterate if you feel like it.

🎨

Create over consume

Instead of scrolling through tutorials, just start building. The best way to learn is to describe something wild and see what the AI produces.

// How it works

The vibecoding workflow

Five steps. No gatekeeping. Anyone can do this.

01

Get an idea (or don't)

Wake up. See a meme. Think "what if that was a website?" Alternatively, grab a prompt from the ai201 daily challenge. The bar is on the floor and that's where we like it.

02

Describe the vibe

Open your AI tool of choice. Tell it what you want. Be specific about aesthetics, weird about functionality, and absolutely unhinged about scope. "Make it look like a 90s Geocities page but for a luxury brand" is a valid prompt.

03

Iterate by feeling

Look at what the AI generated. Does it spark joy? Ship it. Does it feel off? Tell the AI why. "Make the button angrier" is legitimate feedback. Trust your gut, not the linter.

04

Deploy immediately

Don't wait. Don't QA. Don't write tests (controversial, we know). Push it live. The internet is your staging environment. Vercel, Netlify, or just drag the HTML file somewhere — it doesn't matter.

05

Share the chaos

Post it in the ai201 community. Tweet it. Send it to your mum. The whole point is that someone, somewhere, sees your weird little creation and either laughs, is inspired, or is deeply confused. All three are wins.

// The rules

The vibecoder's manifesto

Rule 01

Ship daily, not perfectly

A broken site that exists beats a polished one that doesn't. Your deploy button is your best friend.

Rule 02

Describe, don't debug

If the AI gave you something weird, describe the fix in plain English. "Make it less ugly" is valid feedback.

Rule 03

Weird is a feature

The internet has enough bland landing pages. Make something that makes people say "what?" — that's the goal.

Rule 04

Hype others relentlessly

See someone ship something? Gas them up. The vibecoding community runs on mutual admiration and unhinged encouragement.

Rule 05

No gatekeeping. Ever.

You don't need to know JavaScript. You don't need a CS degree. You need an idea and the courage to make it exist.

Rule 06

The internet should be fun

We're here to bring joy, chaos, and creativity back to the web. Every vibecoded site makes the internet a little less boring.
