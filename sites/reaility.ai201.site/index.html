reaility
/re·ai·li·ty/   |   noun
noun — plural reailities

1. The emergent state of the world as it is increasingly shaped, perceived, and mediated by artificial intelligence; reality as filtered through machine cognition.

We no longer simply observe reality — we inhabit a reaility, contoured by algorithms we cannot see.

2. A condition in which the boundaries between human experience and AI-generated constructs become indistinguishable.

The photograph looked authentic, but it was a product of reaility — synthesised, plausible, and entirely fabricated.

3. The philosophical acknowledgment that intelligence — artificial or otherwise — is now an inseparable component of what we call real.

To deny reaility is to pretend the mirror has no reflection.
Origin

Early 21st century: a portmanteau of reality and AI (artificial intelligence). First attested in digital discourse, c. 2024, denoting the inseparable entanglement of lived experience with machine intelligence.
