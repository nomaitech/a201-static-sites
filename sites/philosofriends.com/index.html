How it works

Philosophy is better done together.

01

A topic is chosen

Each month we announce a philosophical theme — free will, justice, consciousness, the good life — with a short reading to get you thinking beforehand.

02

We gather in London

A relaxed venue, good drinks, and an hour or two to properly dig into an idea. Usually a pub back room or independent bookshop. Always central London.

03

Everyone has a voice

No lectures. No right answers. Just genuinely curious people sharing perspectives, pushing back, and leaving with something to think about.

Beyond the monthly

More ways to gather

🎙️

Monthly themed discussion

Our flagship event. Each month a philosophical theme is announced — free will, justice, consciousness, the good life — with a short reading to get you thinking. We gather, debate, and leave with something to chew on.

🍽️

Smaller dinners

8–10 people, a long table, and nowhere to be. Rotating hosts, deeper conversation, and the kind of discussion that doesn't fit in a pub.

🚶

Walking discussions

Philosophy while moving through a park. Socrates did it, and it turns out motion is good for thinking. Usually 90 minutes, always informal.

☕

One-on-one pairings

Weekly matchmaking between members with shared interests. An hour, a coffee, and a question to start with. No agenda beyond curiosity.

🏕️

Offline Annual Retreat

A weekend outside London, phones off. Longer, uninterrupted thinking — meals together, long walks, and conversations that carry on past midnight without a screen in sight.

🎤

Member talks

10-minute talks from our own members on something they read and found fascinating. No expertise required — just genuine curiosity. Optional Q&A if you want it. A low-stakes way to practise public speaking on ideas that actually matter to you.

⚡

Speed philosophysing

Like speed dating, but for ideas. You get five minutes to dig into a topic with whoever is sitting across from you — then the bell rings and you move seats. Fast, surprising, and oddly electric.
