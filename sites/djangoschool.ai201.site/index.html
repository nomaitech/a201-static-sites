Common Questions

Everything you want
to know.

Do I need Python experience to start? +
Phase 1 covers Python fundamentals from scratch, so no prior Python is required. That said, if you've already written some Python — even basic scripting — you'll move through the early modules faster and have more time to focus on Django.
Is this self-paced or cohort-based? +
Both options are available. The Self-Paced plan lets you work through the curriculum on your own schedule — the material is always available. The Mentorship plan follows a cohort model with weekly live Q&A sessions, so you have a group to progress with and deadlines to keep you accountable.
How much time should I set aside each week? +
Plan for 8–12 hours per week to stay on pace with the 16-week structure. If you're moving faster, the content is there — there's no artificial gating. If life gets busy, you can slow down without penalty since you have lifetime access.
What platform is the course on? +
All course content lives on our custom Django-built platform (yes, we practice what we teach). Video lessons, project templates, code examples, and community access are all in one place. You'll receive login credentials immediately after enrolling.
What if I fall behind? +
Life happens. Your access never expires, so you can pause and pick back up anytime. If you're on the Mentorship plan and miss a cohort, you can join the next live session cycle at no extra cost. We'd rather you finish late than not at all.
Is there job placement support? +
The Mentorship plan includes career support: resume review, LinkedIn profile feedback, and mock interview prep. We don't have a formal job guarantee, but 87% of our graduates report landing a new role or getting promoted within six months of completing the course (based on our 2024 graduate survey).
Why Django instead of FastAPI or Flask? +
Django's "batteries included" philosophy makes it the fastest path from zero to a production application. The ORM, admin, auth, and deployment tooling are all built in. Once you know Django deeply, picking up FastAPI or Flask takes days — not weeks. It's also the most widely used Python web framework in professional environments.
